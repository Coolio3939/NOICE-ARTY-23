THE BASICS OF CYBERBULLYING

So, after clicking this website, I bet you wonder what cyberbullying is.

Well, in basic terms, Cyberbullying is the use of technology to bully somebody.

Undoubtedly cyberbullying can happen to everybody; but this website is specifically made for teenagers, because some of the issues they face related to cyberbullying are unique to their developing minds.

Let me get you ready for some basics, shall I?

- Cyberbullying can happen in many forms, ranging from leaking private info about somebody without their consent to sending mean texts about somebody.

Cyberbullying is quite a serious issue; consequences can range from anxiety to having suicidal thoughts, or actually doing it.

If you are a person on a group chat for example seeing a cyberbully in action, DO NOT join in or DO NOT become a bystander. Instead, straight away report it to a trusted adult.

And finally, to prevent from getting cyberbullied... simply set privacy settings and selective friend requests on social media. Do NOT further engage with the bully.

FACTS AND STATS ON CYBERBULLYING

Let's learn some interesting facts and statistics about cyberbullying!

And by "interesting" I mean distressing facts that confirm cyberbullying is extremely troubling for teenagers.

41% of Boys and Girls aged 13-14 have experienced cyberbullying. Some of you guys here are 14.

54% of Girls aged 15-17 have experienced cyberbullying. Now the reason girls face more cyberbullying than boys is because of sexism. Pretty sad it exists in 2024 but oh well.

Another distressing fact- Approx 90% of teens who face cyberbullying.... are AFRAID TO REPORT IT! In fear of being labeled a "snitch". Frankly speaking, who cares the cyberbully deserves to be reported anyway.

Latching on from the last fact; 13.6% of teenagers who have been cyberbullied have tried to end it all. Yup, think about it, in a cyberbullying-free world this % would be 0.

COMBATTING CYBERBULLYING


Let's learn some key points on how to combat cyberbullying

For one, you can make your accounts private by altering your privacy settings, which also includes selective friend requests and photo tag approvl

Also remember, whenever you want to post stuff online; consider if it leaks any private information. If it does, then DO NOT post it, it doesnt matter if you friends find it funny, a cyberbully will nevertheless exploit it

If someone sends you many mean messages on online messaging apps; DO NOT retaliate against the cyberbully! No that will make everything even worse! Instead, take screenshots

After you take screenshots, report it to a trusted adult. Evidence is crucial so you can make your case strong and have proof that you were indeed a victim
